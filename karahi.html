Karahi:

Karahi, also known as kadai or kadhai, refers to both a type of cooking utensil and the dishes prepared using that utensil. In South Asian and Central Asian cuisine, a karahi is a deep, circular, and wok-like cooking pan made of cast iron or other materials. It has two handles on the sides and is commonly used for deep-frying, stir-frying, and simmering.

Additionally, dishes prepared in a karahi are often named after the utensil itself. Karahi dishes are popular in Indian, Pakistani, and Afghan cuisines. These dishes are characterized by a rich and flavorful preparation method, often involving a mix of spices and ingredients.

Common examples of karahi dishes include:
1.Chicken Karahi: Chicken cooked in the karahi with a blend of spices, tomatoes, green chilies, and coriander.
2.Lamb Karahi: Similar to chicken karahi but with lamb as the main protein.
3.Vegetable Karahi: A vegetarian version, often featuring a mix of fresh vegetables cooked in the karahi with spices.
4.Prawn Karahi: Involves prawns or shrimp cooked with the characteristic karahi spices.
