Fries:

"Fries" typically refers to French fries, which are a popular and widely consumed food made from potatoes. They are typically cut into long, thin strips, deep-fried until they become crispy and golden brown, and then seasoned with salt. French fries are commonly served as a side dish to accompany burgers, sandwiches, or other main courses.

There are various styles and variations of fries, including shoestring fries, steak fries, waffle fries, and curly fries. They can be enjoyed on their own or with a variety of condiments such as ketchup, mayonnaise, or vinegar. French fries are a beloved comfort food enjoyed in many parts of the world.
